Frequently Asked Questions

Intel has committed to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across its global operations and manufacturing facilities by 2040. This includes Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions, with interim targets set for 2025 and 2030 to ensure steady progress toward this ambitious goal.

Intel sources renewable energy through a combination of direct renewable generation, power purchase agreements (PPAs), and renewable energy credits. The company partners with renewable energy providers globally to ensure a substantial portion of its electricity comes from wind, solar, and other clean energy sources.

Conflict-free manufacturing ensures that minerals used in microprocessors and other products are not sourced from conflict regions. Intel was the first major tech company to produce a conflict-free microprocessor, implementing rigorous supply chain audits and traceability standards.

Intel is committed to water restoration by investing in conservation technologies, water recycling systems, and restoration projects in water-stressed regions. The company aims to restore more water globally than it uses in its operations by 2030.

Circular manufacturing focuses on reducing waste and maximizing material reuse throughout the production lifecycle. Intel implements strategies such as material recovery, recycling programs, and designing products with end-of-life considerations to minimize environmental impact.
